## What AtmosFlow Is

AtmosFlow is a structured indoor air quality (IAQ) assessment platform designed for two audiences: credentialed industrial hygienists conducting professional evaluations, and facility managers documenting building conditions for their portfolios.

The platform is a **software tool** — not sampling hardware. Assessors bring their own instruments (or use consumer-grade monitors in facility manager mode). AtmosFlow provides the workflow, scoring logic, causal analysis, and report generation.

AtmosFlow does not perform sampling. It does not replace professional judgment. It does not determine regulatory compliance. It structures the assessment process so that findings are documented consistently, scored against published reference standards, and presented in a format suitable for professional review.

## What AtmosFlow Is Not

- **Not a compliance engine.** OSHA does not regulate indoor air quality and does not have IAQ-specific standards. AtmosFlow does not claim to determine compliance or non-compliance with any regulation.
- **Not a diagnostic tool.** The platform identifies conditions that warrant investigation. It does not diagnose building problems, confirm root causes, or establish exposure causation.
- **Not a substitute for professional evaluation.** Every report generated by AtmosFlow is labeled "Draft — Pending Professional Review" until a credentialed reviewer signs off.
- **Not sampling hardware.** AtmosFlow accepts input from the assessor's instruments. It does not include or sell measurement devices.

## The Core Value Proposition

A typical IAQ assessment involves 3–4 hours of field work followed by 8–20 hours of report preparation. The field data is collected on paper or in spreadsheets, manually compared against published thresholds, and written up in a Word document that varies in quality depending on the assessor's experience and available time.

AtmosFlow compresses the report preparation phase to near-zero by:

1. **Structuring the field walkthrough** as a guided, one-question-at-a-time flow that captures all required data points
2. **Scoring findings deterministically** against published ASHRAE, OSHA, NIOSH, EPA, and WHO benchmarks — the same inputs always produce the same scores
3. **Generating causal pathway hypotheses** by correlating related findings across categories
4. **Producing a consulting-grade report** with executive summary, zone-by-zone findings, recommendations register, sampling plan, and scoring transparency appendix

The assessor walks in, completes the walkthrough, and has a structured report ready for review before leaving the building.

## Platform Version

Current engine version: **v2.3**

Key capabilities:
- Sufficiency-aware scoring (missing data → insufficient, not full credit)
- AIHA worst-zone composite override (Bullock & Ignacio, 2015)
- Ventilation hierarchy per ASHRAE 62.1-2022 (cfm/person → ACH → CO₂)
- Mold separated from composite per AIHA 2020 / IICRC S520
- TVOC instrument discipline (PID lamp energy, calibration gas required)
- Measurement confidence badges (High / Moderate / Low / Insufficient)
- SBS pattern detection from complaint data alone
- Building-type profiles (data center, healthcare, school — scaffolded)
- Dual-mode operation (IH professional / Facility Manager)
- DOCX and PDF export
