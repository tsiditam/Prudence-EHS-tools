# AtmosFlow White Paper — Defensibility Design

## Why Defensibility Matters

An IAQ assessment report may be reviewed years after it was written — by a different professional, an attorney, a regulatory agency, or a court. The report must be defensible not just at the time of writing, but under future scrutiny when the assessor is not present to explain their reasoning.

AtmosFlow is designed from the ground up to produce reports that withstand this level of review.

## Defensibility Architecture

### 1. Deterministic Scoring

Every score is computed from fixed rules applied to captured data. There is no AI judgment, no machine learning model, and no subjective weighting in the scoring path. The same inputs always produce the same outputs.

The scoring rules are published in every report (Appendix B), so any reviewer can independently verify the score by applying the same rules to the same data.

### 2. Standards Version Manifest

Every assessment embeds a frozen snapshot of which standard versions were applied. When ASHRAE publishes an updated edition, the engine version increments and new assessments reference the new standard — but previously scored assessments retain their original manifest.

This prevents the "which version were you using?" challenge in deposition or peer review.

### 3. Fail-Closed Design

When data is missing, the engine does not assume compliance. Categories with insufficient data return "INSUFFICIENT" — not full credit. This was the primary defect corrected in v2.3 and represents a fundamental design principle: the engine should produce less confident, less favorable output when uncertain.

### 4. Evidence Basis Tagging

Every finding in the report carries its evidence basis:
- **Direct-reading measurement** — assessor recorded a value from a calibrated instrument
- **Visual observation** — assessor directly saw, smelled, or detected
- **Occupant report** — complaint-based, not independently verified
- **Facility report** — from building management, not independently verified
- **Inferred correlation** — pattern derived from multiple data points

This prevents the common defensibility failure of presenting inferred conclusions as confirmed observations.

### 5. Restrained Language

The engine enforces language discipline through QA guardrails:

**Prohibited:** "proves," "confirms root cause," "OSHA violation," "OSHA citation," "noncompliant," "unsafe," "guarantees"

**Required:** "is consistent with," "suggests," "may indicate," "would warrant follow-up," "available evidence supports"

Every causal pathway is labeled as a hypothesis requiring confirmation, not a confirmed root-cause determination.

### 6. Measurement Qualification

Every direct-reading measurement is noted as a point-in-time value. The report explicitly states that direct-reading instruments represent conditions at the moment and location sampled and may not represent worst-case, average, or typical conditions.

Visual mold observations always carry: "visual observation only — not confirmed by sampling."

TVOC readings always carry: "screening indicator only — compound-specific identification requires laboratory analysis."

### 7. Confidence Transparency

The measurement confidence badge (High / Moderate / Low / Insufficient) is displayed prominently alongside the composite score but is visually distinct from it. This signals to the reviewer how much weight to place on the numeric result.

A single-point measurement with no temporal context, no occupancy documentation, and no outdoor baseline is labeled "Low Confidence" — regardless of how favorable the reading appears.

### 8. Draft vs. Final State

Every report generated by AtmosFlow is initially labeled "Draft — Pending Professional Review." The report includes a blank reviewer signoff block. Only after a credentialed professional reviews and approves the report does the status change to "Final Reviewed Report."

This prevents unauthorized reliance on unreviewed automated output.

### 9. Instrument Chain of Custody

The pre-survey captures instrument make/model, serial number, calibration date, and calibration status. This information appears in the report's Scope and Methodology section and in the Assessment Transparency panel.

Calibration overdue or unknown triggers a warning in the report and caps measurement confidence.

### 10. Assessment Mode Gating

Three assessment modes gate report generation:
- **Screening:** no numeric composite, no compliance claims, report labeled "SCREENING ONLY"
- **Walkthrough:** requires minimum instrumentation (CO₂, temp, RH)
- **Full Assessment:** requires full instrument suite, calibration documentation

A user cannot produce a "Full Assessment Report" without meeting the data requirements for that classification.

## What AtmosFlow Does Not Claim

1. AtmosFlow does not determine compliance with any regulation
2. AtmosFlow does not diagnose building problems
3. AtmosFlow does not confirm root causes
4. AtmosFlow does not establish exposure causation
5. AtmosFlow does not replace professional judgment by a qualified IH/EHS professional
6. AtmosFlow does not perform laboratory analysis
7. AtmosFlow does not generate legally binding conclusions

These limitations are stated in every report, in the Terms of Service, and in the application's disclaimer modal.

## Escalation System

The platform includes 19 deterministic escalation rules that trigger mandatory "Professional Evaluation Required" banners:

- IICRC S520 Condition 2 (≥10 sq ft) or Condition 3
- CO above 9 ppm sustained
- Any complaint with medical attention sought
- ≥3 complaints in 30 days with overlapping symptoms
- Critical tier on consecutive assessments
- Active construction in pre-1980 building (asbestos risk)
- Pre-1978 building with paint disturbance (lead risk)
- Silica-generating construction activities
- Water damage without documented dry-out

When any trigger fires, the user is routed to a neutral IH directory (random rotation, no paid placements) to find a credentialed professional.
