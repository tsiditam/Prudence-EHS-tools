Section 1.0

Executive Summary

Screening measurements in the occupied 3rd-floor zones show carbon-dioxide concentrations and an indoor–outdoor differential consistent with under-ventilation, a mean formaldehyde concentration above the NIOSH recommended exposure limit, and total-VOC levels approaching the Mølhave advisory tier. These indicators coincide with a documented occupant symptom cluster and visible moisture at an air-handler drain pan.

Overall Professional Opinion

Screening-level indicators are consistent with a ventilation deficiency compounded by a localized HVAC moisture/hygiene concern. The pattern is sufficient to recommend immediate corrective action and targeted confirmatory sampling. This is a screening-level professional opinion and is not a compliance determination; it requires review and sign-off by a Certified Industrial Hygienist before it is relied upon for remediation or regulatory decisions.

Section 2.0

Summary of Findings

Ventilation deficiency
Indoor CO₂ in occupied zones reached 1,180–1,450 ppm against a 415 ppm outdoor baseline (Δ765–1,035 ppm), exceeding the Δ700 ppm ventilation-screening benchmark. CO₂ is used here as a ventilation indicator, not a contaminant limit.
Benchmark · ASHRAE 62.1-2025 (screening)
Formaldehyde above NIOSH REL
Mean formaldehyde of 0.039 ppm exceeds the NIOSH REL of 0.016 ppm. New furnishings and reduced outdoor-air dilution are plausible contributors; passive-badge TWA sampling is recommended to confirm.
Benchmark · NIOSH REL 0.016 ppm
HVAC moisture / hygiene
Standing water observed in an air-handler drain pan with ceiling-tile staining near returns; maintenance deferred >12 months. Warrants evaluation for microbial amplification and Legionella management per ASHRAE 188.
Reference · ASHRAE 188 · IICRC S500
Occupant symptom cluster
6–10 occupants reported headache, fatigue, and eye irritation that reportedly resolve away from the building — a temporal pattern consistent with a building-related exposure. Pattern, not causation; documentation recommended.
Method · NIOSH IEQ questionnaire

Section 4.0

Zone Findings

3rd Floor Open Office~35 occupants · 6–10 symptomatic · Central AHU (VAV)
Under-ventilation indicated by CO₂ differentialCritical concern
Observed
1,180 ppm indoor CO₂ vs. 415 ppm outdoor (Δ765 ppm), exceeding the Δ700 ppm ventilation-screening benchmark during occupied hours.
Limitations
CO₂ is a surrogate for outdoor-air adequacy, not a contaminant. Spot readings; not a time-weighted average. Damper position and VAV box minimums were not directly metered.
Recommended actions
  • Inspect outdoor-air damper operation and VAV minimum-position setpoints.
  • Measure delivered outdoor-air rate (balometer) against ASHRAE 62.1 design intent.
  • Replace heavily loaded filters; verify economizer function.
Formaldehyde above NIOSH RELElevated
Observed
0.039 ppm mean (range 0.031–0.046 ppm) vs. NIOSH REL 0.016 ppm. Outdoor reference <0.005 ppm.
Limitations
Direct-reading screening value, not a validated TWA. Off-gassing varies with temperature, humidity, and material age; a single visit cannot establish chronic exposure.
Recommended actions
  • Collect an 8-hour passive-badge TWA sample (NIOSH 2016) during occupied conditions, with an outdoor background and a field blank.
  • Increase outdoor-air dilution; identify recent furnishing/finish installations.
Conference Room B~12 occupants · 3–5 symptomatic · No dedicated OA supply
HVAC moisture & hygiene concernModerate concern
Observed
1,450 ppm peak CO₂ during an occupied meeting; standing water in the serving air-handler drain pan with ceiling-tile staining and a musty odor near returns.
Limitations
Visual/olfactory observation only; microbial amplification was not confirmed by sampling. Spore counts, if later collected, are not in themselves proof of a health effect.
Recommended actions
  • Arrest the moisture source and remediate the drain pan per IICRC S500; evaluate for Legionella management under a Water Management Program (ASHRAE 188).
  • Collect non-culturable spore-trap air samples (Air-O-Cell) with a concurrent outdoor control.

Section 6.0

Limitations & Professional Sign-Off

This is a screening-level assessment. It identifies risk indicators by comparing direct-reading measurements and visual observations against published benchmarks. It does not constitute a compliance determination, a certified measurement, a definitive identification of a contaminant source, or professional industrial-hygiene advice in place of a qualified review.

Measurements represent point-in-time conditions on the assessment date and may not reflect conditions under different occupancy, weather, or HVAC operating states. Direct-reading instruments provide screening-grade values, not laboratory-validated, time-weighted exposures. Benchmark types carry different legal and technical weight and are not interchangeable; CO₂ is reported as a ventilation indicator rather than a contaminant limit, and TVOC is referenced to an advisory tier for which no consensus health limit exists.

Conclusions are contingent on the confirmatory sampling identified in the Recommendations Register. This draft requires review and sign-off by a Certified Industrial Hygienist (CIH) before it is relied upon for remediation, regulatory, or litigation purposes.
